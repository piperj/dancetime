Recommendations for Johan

Quick Win — American Smooth
American Smooth — the clearest win for Johan. With G/AC categories at ~12% contested vs Int'l Ballroom at ~0%, American Smooth is roughly 7× more likely to have a rival in the same bracket. Syllabus overlaps heavily with Int'l Standard.
Stay Int'l → Add Latin
Int'l Latin over Ballroom. Within Int'l, Cha Cha and Rumba at ~20% contested are the best options — still thin, but the gap with Ballroom is real. Jive and Samba follow.
Silver → More Contested
Silver doesn't fix the problem. The G/AC Gentleman categories are sparse at every level — moving to Silver doesn't open up new rivals unless more Gentleman competitors show up. The style choice matters far more than the level.

Ballroom vs Latin vs American — Chance of a Contested Heat

In American Smooth, roughly 1-in-2 heats has a rival in the same age group. Int'l Latin is around 1-in-3; Int'l Ballroom closer to 1-in-4.

Contested Rate by Level — Bronze + Silver

Bronze — Bronze Challenge and Pre-Bronze lead (~44–50%). Silver — Full Silver (~44%) and Pre-Silver (~41%) are just as contested. Moving from Full Bronze to Silver doesn't thin the field; it maintains it.

Int'l — Chance of a Contested Heat by Dance

Cha Cha leads at ~40% contested — the most reliable way to face a rival in Int'l. Paso Doble is the thinnest at ~21%. For Ballroom, Waltz and Foxtrot edge out Tango and Quickstep.
